6III. READING – Part 3: Reading Comprehension (Câu 71–80)

Chọn phương án đúng nhất (A, B, C, hoặc D) cho mỗi câu hỏi sau.

The History of Human Communication

Throughout human history, the movement of information has been shaped by the tools available for communication. In early societies, messages were carried by runners, riders, or ships, and the speed of communication depended largely on physical distance and weather conditions. Important announcements could take weeks or even months to reach distant regions. The invention of writing represented a major step forward, as it allowed laws, trade agreements, and historical records to be preserved more accurately than oral memory could manage. However, written messages still had to be transported by human or animal labor.

A dramatic change occurred with the development of printing in the fifteenth century. Before movable type became widespread in Europe, books were usually copied by hand, making them expensive and rare. Printing made it possible to reproduce texts in far greater numbers and at lower cost. As a result, religious ideas, scientific discoveries, and political arguments circulated more rapidly than ever before. Although printed material did not eliminate social inequality, it gave a growing number of people access to knowledge that had previously been limited to small educated groups. In this sense, printing helped transform information from a privilege into a more public resource.

By the nineteenth century, the telegraph introduced an even more radical shift. For the first time, messages could travel faster than people or goods. A businessperson in one city could receive market information from another city almost immediately, and newspapers could report distant events with unprecedented speed. The telephone later added the human voice to long-distance communication, allowing conversations to occur across large spaces. These inventions did not merely make communication quicker; they also changed people's expectations. Delays that had once seemed unavoidable began to appear inefficient or unacceptable.

In the late twentieth century, digital networks accelerated this transformation further. Email, mobile phones, and the internet reduced the cost of communication and enabled ordinary users to send information instantly across national borders. Social media platforms later allowed individuals to publish news, opinions, and images to large audiences without depending on traditional publishers. This development created new opportunities for education, activism, and cultural exchange. At the same time, it also made misinformation easier to spread. Unlike printed books or newspapers, online messages can be copied, altered, and shared by millions before their accuracy is checked.

Today, societies face the challenge of managing a world in which information is abundant but not always reliable. New technologies have made communication faster and more democratic, yet they have also placed greater responsibility on individuals to evaluate what they read and share. The history of communication, therefore, is not simply a story of progress from slow to fast. It is also a story of how each new medium changes the relationship between knowledge, power, and public judgment.

✍️ V. WRITING – Part 2: Paragraph Writing  (Gợi ý – Không tính điểm)
📝 Phần viết đoạn văn: Thực hành tự do. Đáp án mẫu và gợi ý được cung cấp phía dưới. Không tính điểm tự động.
Đề bài: Many students believe that learning a foreign language is more useful than learning how to play a musical instrument.
Write a paragraph of about 140–160 words expressing your opinion on this view.
ℹ️ Phần này chỉ hiển thị gợi ý. Viết bài của bạn vào ô bên dưới để luyện tập.
💡 Gợi ý cấu trúc đoạn văn (nhấn để mở)

Cấu trúc đoạn văn Opinion Paragraph (140–160 từ):

  • Topic sentence: Nêu rõ quan điểm của bạn (đồng ý / không đồng ý / đồng ý một phần).
  • Supporting idea 1 + Example: Lý do thứ nhất ủng hộ quan điểm. Ví dụ cụ thể.
  • Supporting idea 2 + Example: Lý do thứ hai. Ví dụ cụ thể.
  • Concession (optional): Thừa nhận điểm mạnh của quan điểm đối lập.
  • Concluding sentence: Tóm tắt lại quan điểm của bạn.

Từ vựng gợi ý: practical skills, employability, cross-cultural understanding, cognitive development, emotional outlet, discipline, perseverance, bilateral brain stimulation, lingua franca, globalization

Đáp án mẫu (quan điểm đồng ý một phần):
While I acknowledge that learning a foreign language offers undeniable practical advantages in today's globalized world, I believe the comparison oversimplifies what education should achieve. Mastering a foreign language clearly opens doors to wider employment opportunities, facilitates cross-cultural communication, and enhances cognitive flexibility. For instance, students proficient in English or Mandarin gain access to international job markets that their monolingual peers cannot. However, learning a musical instrument cultivates equally vital qualities: emotional discipline, creative thinking, and perseverance under pressure. Research suggests that musical training strengthens neural pathways involved in memory and abstract reasoning, skills that benefit academic performance across subjects. Therefore, rather than viewing these two pursuits as rivals, schools and students would do well to recognize them as complementary investments in human development. The ideal is not to choose one over the other, but to appreciate how each uniquely enriches the learner.
